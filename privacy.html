Aurum Task Desk — privacy policy

This policy explains, in plain language, how Aurum Task Desk (“the desk”, “we”, “us”) handles information when you visit the site, use the Desk, or contact the team.

This is a transparency page aimed at older, non-technical users. It is not a contract or legal advice, but it does describe how the service is intended to behave in practice.

1. What this desk is (and is not)

Aurum Task Desk is a private AI-assisted writing service for serious letters. You type in a situation, the desk helps you structure and draft a letter. We do not run social feeds, do not sell advertisement space, and do not sell your letters as “content”.

The service uses external infrastructure (such as hosting, email and payment providers, and AI model providers) to function, but the desk is designed around a low-drama, low-exposure mindset.

2. Information you type into the Desk

When you use the Desk to generate a draft, the text you type into the form (for example, what happened, timeline, people involved) is sent to our backend worker and to one or more AI model providers so a draft letter can be generated.

  • We use this information only to process your request and improve the immediate quality of that draft.
  • We do not publish your drafts or use them as marketing material without your explicit consent.
  • You are encouraged to remove full card numbers, passwords and similar secrets from the text, or add those later in your own document before sending.

3. Accounts, login, and support

Some parts of the Desk may allow or require you to create an account (for example, to view order history or manage access to multiple letters).

  • If you register, we store only the minimum account data needed (such as email, login records and basic preferences) to keep your access stable and secure.
  • When you write to support (for example, via support@aurumdesks.com), your email and any attachments will be stored in the support inbox so we can respond.

4. Payments and billing information

When you pay for a letter, task pack or subscription, your payment is handled by a third-party payment provider. This may include online payment platforms or card processors.

  • The Desk itself does not see or store your full card number or bank password. That information is handled by the payment provider.
  • We may receive limited billing information such as transaction IDs, amounts, currency, and status (success / failed / refunded) to keep records and provide support.

5. Logs, cookies and analytics

Like most websites, the Desk keeps basic technical logs to keep the service secure and stable. These may include your IP address, browser type, and timestamps of requests.

The site may also use analytics tools (for example, Google Analytics) to understand which pages are visited and how often people successfully complete a task. These tools typically set cookies in your browser.

  • Analytics are used in aggregate form to improve the Desk (for example, spotting a page where many people get stuck).
  • We do not use analytics to build “interest profiles” for advertising on other websites.

6. How long information is kept

Different types of information are kept for different lengths of time, depending on why they exist:

  • Technical logs are usually kept for a limited period (for example, weeks or a few months) to investigate abuse and maintain security.
  • Basic billing records may be kept longer where law or accounting rules require it.
  • Support emails are kept as part of the support history so we can follow up on previous issues, unless you specifically request deletion where law allows.

7. Where data may be processed

Because the Desk uses international infrastructure (hosting, AI models, payment processors, email services), your information may be processed in countries other than your own.

We aim to work only with providers who have a reasonable reputation for security and compliance. However, the exact legal protections available to you may depend on your country and the provider’s location.

8. Your choices and rights

Depending on where you live, you may have legal rights to access, correct, or delete certain information about you, or to object to certain uses.

  • You can choose how much detail to include in prompts. You can also keep your own copies of drafts on your devices and delete any local browser data.
  • If you wish to exercise a data right (for example, ask what data we hold that is linked to your email), you can write to support@aurumdesks.com. We may ask you to verify your identity before responding.

9. Changes to this policy

The Desk may update this privacy policy from time to time as the service, technology or law changes. The updated version will be posted on this page with a revised date.

If a change seems significant for ordinary users, we will aim to highlight it in a short note or banner rather than hiding it in small print.

Plain-language summary

For older professionals who dislike drama, here is the short version of how privacy works here:
  • ● You type a situation; the Desk and its AI providers use it to generate a draft letter. We are not in the business of selling your letters as “content”.
  • ● Payments go through outside payment platforms. They see your card details; we mostly see the result and receipts.
  • ● Analytics are used to see which pages work, not to follow you around the internet with ads.
  • ● If you are worried or want to know what data is linked to your email, you can calmly write to the support inbox and ask.
Last updated: 2025. This page is meant to be readable, not intimidating. If there is a conflict between this summary and any mandatory local law, the local law will override where required.
